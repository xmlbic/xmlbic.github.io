A Quiet Beginning to 2026


```

It started quietly.

No fireworks.

No loud voices counting down to something new.

Just a night that felt… the same.

And a few prayers, whispered without knowing if they were meant to be heard.


Another year

It sounds simple when you say it out loud.

But it does not feel simple anymore.

Age arrives differently now.

Not as a number, but as small reminders.

The body asking for more rest.

The mind asking for more certainty.

And somehow, both are harder to give.


So how do I begin this year?

With the same resolutions I keep repeating,

as if repetition alone will make them real?

With the same hopes,

wearing a slightly different shape,

but carrying the same weight?


I keep asking myself what it means to become better

Is it about fixing everything that feels wrong,

or finally accepting that not everything will?

There were too many things this past year.

People who came,

and people who left,

sometimes without warning,

sometimes without closure.

There were beginnings that did not last,

and endings that stayed longer than they should have.

And somewhere in between,

I kept trying to hold everything together.


I am not sure if I did

They say acceptance is the answer.

To let go.

To be patient.

To be strong.

But no one really tells you

how heavy those words can feel

when you actually try to live them.


Time does not wait

It does not care if you are ready,

or tired,

or still figuring things out.

It moves anyway.

And all you can do

is move with it,

even when you are unsure where it is taking you.


So maybe this is how I begin

Not with certainty.

Not with a new version of myself.

Just… continuing.

Doing what I can when I am strong.

Holding on to something when I am not.

And learning, slowly,

that maybe that is enough.


Because the year will change

It always does.

But nothing really shifts

unless I do.

And even then,

it happens quietly.

Almost unnoticed.

Almost like this beginning.

```
